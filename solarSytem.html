Our solar system includes the Sun, eight planets, five officially named dwarf planets,
hundreds of moons, and thousands of asteroids and comets.

Key Facts About the Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system.
It provides the energy that sustains life on Earth.
  • Type: G-type main-sequence star (Yellow Dwarf).
  • Mass: About 99.8% of the total mass of the solar system.
  • Diameter: Approximately 1.39 million km.
  • Core: Around 15 million °C.
  • Surface: About 5,500 °C.
  • Composition: Mainly Hydrogen (H, ~74%) and Helium (He, ~24%).
  • Energy Source: Nuclear fusion of hydrogen into helium.
